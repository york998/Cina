Find traffic accidents' causes

Analyzing the cause of the traffic accident based on data mining

This work focuses on building a model named >the cause of the traffic accident analysis model. This model mainly combines two data mining method. The first one is a cluster analysis method, called greedy algorithm based on modularity, and the other one is association rules. In this model, the function of the first step is to classify the whole traffic accident data, to find out the different kind of traffic accident and to prepare for the next step. In the second step, the association rules are using to dig out more information, which is hiding behind large number of data. This model has a great advantage when compare to the traditional ways. It can save more time and space in computer memory, and also allow more people to involve more factors when analyzing the main and related factors. This study uses the traffic accident data from Guiyang City of Guizhou Province in China. In this case, we examine the practicability of the cause of the traffic accident analysis model. It finally turns out that the process of analyzing these plenty of data, is much quicker and cost less computer memory and also the information, we digging out, is more detailed.

  • Date: May 2016
  • Client: City of Guiyang Department of Transportation
  • Category: Data Mining

Conflict Dynamics in Wikipedia

Conflict dynamic in Wikipedia editing: the role of contingently activated ZEALOTS.

In this work, a variant of Torok et al.'s model is proposed, based on the hypothesis that the contingent-activation of zealots with extremist opinions is substitutable to the agent-renewal mechanism in generating controversies. The substitutability is partly supported by numerical simulations, which basically reproduce the transitions between the three regimes of "single conflict", "plateaus of consensus", and "uninterrupted controversy", as same as in Torok et al.'s model.

  • Client: China Minsheng Bank Shenyang Branch
